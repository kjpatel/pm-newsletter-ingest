# 5 Ways To Get Product Discovery Wrong

**Source**: [Itamar Gilad](https://itamargilad.com/discovery-problems/)
**Author**: Itamar Gilad | **Date**: Mar 10, 2026

---

## Summary

Many companies fail at product discovery despite understanding its importance, falling into five common traps: treating ideas as must-haves to skip validation, setting weak or missing goals, lacking discovery infrastructure, performing partial validation, and failing to learn from experiments. The article emphasizes that product discovery requires systemic changes and leadership buy-in to overcome these barriers.

## Key Takeaways

- **Eliminate the two-track system** where some features bypass discovery as "must-haves." Require most ideas to go through product discovery and only circumvent it for isolated, high-impact business opportunities or legal/compliance threats.
- **Demonstrate business value of discovery** to managers by quantifying waste from failed "must-have" features in terms of dollars, person-weeks, and competitive disadvantage to build organizational support.
- **Build trust through transparent sharing** of interim discovery results, experiments, and evidence-based decisions using communication tools like GIST boards to help stakeholders understand that reality is unpredictable and validation reduces risk.
- **Establish clear, measurable team goals** at the product level that go beyond company-level business objectives, since teams need specific outcome targets to guide discovery work and prevent roadmap-driven development.
- **Create infrastructure for discovery** by allocating dedicated resources, tools, and processes for conducting research and experimentation rather than treating discovery as an afterthought squeezed between planning and delivery.
