## Summary

HubSpot co-founder and Sequoia CEO coach Brian Halligan argues that while AI has made starting companies easier than ever, scaling remains the critical challenge, requiring strong founding teams, unconventional hiring practices, and leadership frameworks like his LOCKS model.

## Key Takeaways

- **Apply the LOCKS framework** when evaluating founders—focus on their specific leadership qualities rather than consensus-based hiring to identify exceptional founders capable of scaling.
- **Build teams like the 2004 Red Sox** by hiring "spicy" candidates with strong opinions and complementary skills instead of picking consensus candidates, creating more dynamic and innovative organizations.
- **Prepare for enterprise sales as AI's last frontier** since relationship-based, consultative sales will be among the last white-collar jobs automated, making this a sustainable competitive advantage for B2B companies.
- **Recognize the asymmetry between starting and scaling** — use AI and modern tools to accelerate company formation, but invest heavily in team building and leadership development as the real differentiator for scale.
