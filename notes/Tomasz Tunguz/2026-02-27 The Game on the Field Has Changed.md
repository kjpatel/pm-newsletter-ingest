# The Game on the Field Has Changed

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/are-we-seeing-layoffs-from-ai/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Feb 27, 2026

---

## Summary

Major tech companies are conducting significant layoffs while maintaining growth, driven by AI-enabled productivity gains that allow companies to operate with substantially fewer employees and achieve dramatically higher revenue per employee ratios. This shift is fundamentally changing efficiency expectations across the industry, with AI-native companies achieving 20-40x higher revenue per employee than traditional benchmarks.

## Key Takeaways

- **Audit your headcount efficiency**: Calculate your current revenue per employee and benchmark against AI-native competitors (targeting $2-4M ARR per employee) to identify if your team is properly leveraged.
- **Implement AI tools strategically**: Deploy AI coding assistants and automation platforms across your organization to match the productivity multipliers that companies like Block and Amazon are achieving.
- **Expect investor pressure on metrics**: Update your financial models and pitch materials to reflect the new efficiency standards (2x productivity expectations) or risk appearing uncompetitive to VCs and board members.
- **Seize individual leverage opportunities**: As an IC or manager, this is the optimal time to demonstrate outsized impact through AI-powered work, positioning yourself for promotion and influence during structural reorganizations.
- **Plan proactive restructuring**: Don't wait to be forced into reactive cuts like competitors—conduct an honest assessment of whether your company can operate with 30-40% fewer people, and if so, move deliberately rather than reactively.
