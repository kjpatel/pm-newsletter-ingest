# Why Saudi Aramco Isn't a Proxy for SpaceX

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/largest-ipos-market-cap-and-float/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Feb 25, 2026

---

## Summary

Saudi Aramco's massive IPO is not a reliable precedent for mega-IPOs like SpaceX because it was designed to serve strategic government goals rather than provide genuine market liquidity, making Alibaba a more relevant comparison for understanding how trillion-dollar IPOs might actually perform.

## Key Takeaways

- **Examine float percentage carefully**: Saudi Aramco only floated 1.5% of the company and remains at 2.4%, whereas true market tests like Alibaba expanded float from 15% to 86%, making float size a critical metric for IPO viability.
- **Plan for post-IPO lockup unlocks**: Monitor the 180-day lockup period expiration, as Alibaba's September 2015 unlock caused a 52% stock decline by flooding the market; companies like SpaceX may have less pressure due to prior tender offers.
- **Use tender offers as pre-IPO pressure valves**: SpaceX, OpenAI, and Anthropic have already allowed significant selling through regular tender offers (SpaceX 2-3x yearly, Anthropic's $350b offering), which may reduce post-IPO selling pressure compared to historical precedents.
- **Distinguish strategic IPOs from liquidity IPOs**: Saudi Aramco's IPO achieved geopolitical and financial goals beyond capital raising, making it a poor proxy for understanding market dynamics of a trillion-dollar IPO where liquidity is the primary objective.
- **Recognize unprecedented market scale challenges**: No public market has previously absorbed a trillion-dollar IPO focused on liquidity, making historical precedents like Alibaba ($231b) only a partial guide for what SpaceX might face.
