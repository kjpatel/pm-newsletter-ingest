# Hello, Claude? Are You There?

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/what-if-we-run-out-of-capacity/)

## Summary

AI infrastructure constraints—including GPUs, power, data centers, and memory—will remain critically tight through 2028, forcing enterprises to move away from freely available inference toward rationing models, rising prices, and optimization of workloads.

## Key Takeaways

- **Plan for inference rationing**: Organizations should prepare for a world where on-demand access to state-of-the-art AI models becomes limited and rationed across teams based on business priority.
- **Expect inference price increases**: Static inference pricing will rise as subsidies become harder to justify, requiring enterprises to budget for higher AI operational costs.
- **Optimize workload selection**: Conduct an inventory of which business functions actually require frontier trillion-parameter models versus smaller, more efficient alternatives.
- **Adopt open-source and smaller models**: Shift non-critical workloads to open-source solutions and smaller models to conserve access to premium inference capacity for highest-impact use cases.
- **Build constraint-driven innovation**: Use infrastructure scarcity as a forcing function to innovate in efficiency, prompting teams to achieve more with less computational resources.
