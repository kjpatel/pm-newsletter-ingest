# The Pricing Power of Agents

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/observations-about-agent-pricing/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Mar 20, 2026

---

## Summary

AI agents are commanding 75-100% of human salaries in labor-shortage markets, with second and third-order benefits including faster training, reduced management overhead, and significantly lower tax burden (25-30% savings), creating a massive TAM expansion as labor costs shift to software.

## Key Takeaways

- **Price agents at parity with human salaries** in labor-shortage markets where urgency to hire is high, as vendors have pricing power and aren't competing on cost per agent.
- **Capture second-order benefits in your pitch** including 24/7 availability, faster training through parallel data presentation, and reduced management overhead to justify agent deployment.
- **Emphasize tax advantages** to CFOs and finance teams—agents save 25-30% compared to human employees due to no FICA, unemployment insurance, or benefits, with software costs being tax-deductible.
- **Prepare for organizational design reviews** when usage surges faster than anticipated; this is when customers pause to restructure, so have a playbook ready for this critical moment.
- **Capitalize on the macro shift** from labor to software—as labor's GDP share hits record lows and low-labor-cost stocks outperform, every dollar shifted from labor to software improves margins and creates expansion opportunities.
