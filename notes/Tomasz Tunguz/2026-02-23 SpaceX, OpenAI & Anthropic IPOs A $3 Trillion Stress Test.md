# SpaceX, OpenAI & Anthropic IPOs : A $3 Trillion Stress Test

**Source**: [Tomasz Tunguz](https://www.tomtunguz.com/spacex-openai-anthropic-ipo-2026/)
**Author**: Tomasz Tunguz | **Date**: Feb 23, 2026

## Summary

Three unprecedented mega-IPOs (SpaceX, OpenAI, Anthropic) totaling ~$2.9 trillion in market cap will force companies to debut with historically small public floats (3-8%), creating massive market disruption when they eventually qualify for S&P 500 inclusion and trigger forced buying by passive index funds.

## Key Takeaways

- **Understand float mechanics**: Standard 15-25% floats would require $432-576B raised in a single quarter—more than the entire US IPO market raised in a decade—forcing these companies to debut with 3-8% floats instead.
- **Monitor S&P 500 index inclusion timelines**: When SpaceX or OpenAI reach the required 50% public float threshold, passive funds managing $20T will be forced to buy, triggering cascading selling pressure on existing mega-cap holdings.
- **Prepare for momentum-driven volatility**: The self-reinforcing cycle of index fund rebalancing will push existing mega-caps (Apple, Microsoft, NVIDIA) lower, triggering momentum strategies that amplify downward pressure across the market.
- **Plan capital allocation accordingly**: This structural market disruption will create both significant risks and opportunities—position defensively in mega-caps facing index selling pressure or opportunistically in dislocated assets.
