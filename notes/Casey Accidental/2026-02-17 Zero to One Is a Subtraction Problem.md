# Zero to One Is a Subtraction Problem

**Source**: [Casey Accidental](https://www.caseyaccidental.com/p/zero-to-one-subtraction)
**Author**: Casey Winters | **Date**: Feb 17, 2026

---

## Summary

Zero to one success is fundamentally about strategic pruning rather than rapid building, requiring founders to understand their product vision and distinguish between distracting features and those that compound value with network density.

## Key Takeaways

- **Map your founder axes**: Explicitly decide whether you're operating in a maximal or minimal features/design quadrant, as each approach has different learning curves, positioning strength, and pruning burdens that affect how you should operate.
- **Implement formal experiment reviews**: After each feature launches, conduct a structured review asking whether it's a hit (requiring roadmap reprioritization), a distraction to kill, or a compounder worth iterating on or deferring until higher density.
- **Define what you need to own**: Ground feature decisions in your long-term vision by asking what the company must own, wants to compete to win, wants to attract, and wants to reject—this clarity enables fast, confident pruning.
- **Distinguish compounders from distractions**: Recognize that in networks, the line between these is blurry; features become compounders only if they strengthen your core value prop and network density, not just engagement metrics.
