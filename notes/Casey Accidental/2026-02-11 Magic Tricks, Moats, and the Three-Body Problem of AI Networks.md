# Magic Tricks, Moats, and the Three-Body Problem of AI Networks

**Source**: [Casey Accidental](https://www.caseyaccidental.com/p/magic-tricks-moats-and-the-three)
**Author**: Casey Winters | **Date**: Feb 11, 2026

---

## Summary

Most AI products today are 'magic tricks' that go viral through novel technology but lack defensible moats, and new consumer network businesses won't emerge at scale until AI creates new user time/attention (new land) and founders solve the three-body problem of aligning target audience, network size, and value proposition simultaneously.

## Key Takeaways

- **Distinguish between viral magic tricks and defensible moats**: Build products architected for defensibility from day one rather than optimizing for short-term growth metrics that mask weak underlying fundamentals—the companies that endured from mobile (Instagram, TikTok, Uber) came years later, not from early viral hits.
- **Solve the network three-body problem systematically**: When tuning a consumer network, simultaneously diagnose and iterate on target audience, network size, and value proposition rather than defaulting to acquisition spend—misdiagnosing churn as a density problem instead of a value problem will drain your budget.
- **Wait for structural unlocks, not just capability gains**: AI's current capability increases haven't created new land (free user time/attention like mobile did); look for regulatory or infrastructure shifts that create cheap distribution channels, as the gambling deregulation wave demonstrates.
- **Resist early monetization pressure**: Monetizing too early (to cover compute costs) masks weak retention and forces act-one scaling over act-two defensibility—sustainable network businesses need patience to build density before attempting revenue.
