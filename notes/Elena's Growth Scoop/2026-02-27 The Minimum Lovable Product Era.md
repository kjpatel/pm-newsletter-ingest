# The Minimum Lovable Product Era

**Source**: [Elena's Growth Scoop](https://www.elenaverna.com/p/the-minimum-lovable-product-era)
**Author**: Elena Verna | **Date**: Feb 27, 2026

---

## Summary

The MVP (Minimum Viable Product) framework is obsolete in an AI-enabled world where development costs have collapsed and functionality has become commoditized. The new standard should be MLP (Minimum Lovable Product)—products that delight users emotionally and create defensible moats through personality, taste, and human connection rather than mere functionality.

## Key Takeaways

- **Shift from functional to emotional**: Stop optimizing for 'barely works' and start designing products that make users feel something. Lovability is now the last defensible moat against commoditized features.
- **Embed brand personality into product decisions**: Every interaction—error handling, celebrations, tone, design choices—should reflect your brand's emotional layer. This can't be a marketing veneer; it must be baked into core product architecture.
- **Design 'love marks' intentionally**: Include unexpected, delightful moments of humanity that add zero functional value but drive retention, LTV, and word-of-mouth (Superhuman's inbox zero celebration, Spotify's AI DJ personality).
- **Rethink the hierarchy of product needs**: Stack functionality, reliability, and usability, but make lovability the baseline. Most products stall at the bottom two layers; MLPs reach the emotional tier that converts users to evangelists.
- **Apply MLP logic even in B2B/enterprise**: Lovability isn't just for consumer apps. Enterprise users crave delight too; dismissing emotional design as 'not serious enough' is leaving competitive advantage on the table.
