## Summary

Nadav Abrahami, co-founder of Wix, shares a masterclass on properly using AI prototyping tools by following a structured workflow that emphasizes problem space work before solution building, divergent exploration, and end-to-end user testing. Most PMs misuse these tools by skipping discovery and validation steps, but the right sequencing can dramatically accelerate product development.

## Key Takeaways

- **Lock down three elements before prototyping**: Define the specific problem, user story, and rough solution shape—if you can't write all three in one paragraph, return to discovery work first.
- **Build 3-4 divergent solutions at ideation**: Leverage AI's speed to explore multiple variations quickly; the comparison between versions provides more learning value than perfecting a single direction.
- **Use visual editing for fine-tuning, not prompting**: Switch from AI prompts to direct visual editing (moving elements, adjusting colors/spacing) for immediate feedback and faster iteration on structural details.
- **Test with actual users who requested the feature**: Skip formal user testing platforms and instead conduct quick video calls with your power users to validate whether you solved their real problem in 20 minutes.
- **Audit prompts for ambiguity before execution**: Use an LLM to find contradictions and unclear language in complex prompts, then use discuss/plan mode to confirm the AI's understanding before running the full request.
