# There's a New PM Skill. It's Called Taste at Speed

**Source**: [Product Growth](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/taste-at-speed)
**Author**: Aakash Gupta | **Date**: Mar 13, 2026

---

## Summary

The article introduces 'taste at speed' as the defining PM skill in the AI era—the ability to rapidly evaluate working prototypes, kill inferior versions, and ship the best ones. As AI tools commoditize software development, PMs must shift from lengthy pre-build specifications to real-time judgment on working software, fundamentally changing how product teams operate.

## Key Takeaways

- **Shift from PRDs to prototypes**: Move specifications from the planning phase to after prototype evaluation. Test working software in hours instead of weeks, then spec the survivor rather than specifying before building.
- **Build judgment through volume**: Evaluate 15 prototypes weekly instead of one spec monthly to compound your taste gap over 6 months. Speed creates a flywheel where experience gap becomes taste gap becomes career gap.
- **Embrace the 80% kill rate**: Use AI tools to generate divergent options (5+ parallel prototypes simultaneously), then ruthlessly eliminate weak ideas. Speed without filtering judgment just builds wrong things faster.
- **Adopt cyclical team workflows**: Replace linear Idea→PRD→Design→Build→Ship with cyclical Idea→5 prototypes→Evaluate→Kill 4→Spec→Ship. Organize teams around parallel exploration rather than sequential approval.
- **Democratize building across roles**: Enable PMs, designers, and non-technical staff to generate prototypes using Claude Code and similar tools. This shifts the bottleneck from 'can we build it' to 'should we ship it.'
