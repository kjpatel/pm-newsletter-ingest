# How to Price AI Products: The Complete Guide for PMs

**Source**: [Product Growth](https://www.news.aakashg.com/p/how-to-price-ai-products)
**Author**: Aakash Gupta (featuring Moe Ali) | **Date**: Feb 26, 2026

---

## Summary

AI product pricing is fundamentally different from traditional SaaS due to variable compute costs, and leading AI companies are adopting six distinct pricing models—often in combination—each with different trade-offs between margin protection and user trust.

## Key Takeaways

- **Map your unit economics first**: AI products have 20-60% gross margins vs. 70-90% for traditional SaaS because every user action incurs real compute costs. Calculate cost-per-user at each pricing tier before launching to avoid margin disasters like Replit's swing to -14%.
- **Avoid intentional opacity in usage-based pricing**: While OpenAI and Anthropic deliberately hide exact usage limits for flexibility, this erodes user trust. Cursor's $7,225 invoice backlash proves that transparency on variable costs prevents customer revolts.
- **Credit/token pool models require over-communication**: When switching from flat-rate to variable-cost models, explicitly explain what depletes credits and at what rate. Cursor's failure to communicate led to a public CEO apology—establish clear expectations upfront.
- **Hybrid pricing is now standard, not optional**: Nearly half of top 50 AI startups use 2-3 pricing models simultaneously (e.g., OpenAI's subscription + API + freemium). Design your go-to-market around multiple models rather than betting on a single approach.
- **Use product analytics to track profitability by segment**: Different user cohorts have vastly different compute costs. Monitor cost-per-user across tiers and pricing models continuously to catch margin degradation before it becomes a crisis.
