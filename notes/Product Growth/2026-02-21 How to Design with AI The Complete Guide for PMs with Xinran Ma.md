## Summary

This guide breaks down how PMs can leverage AI design tools to rapidly prototype experiences without needing deep design expertise, covering five core categories from prompting to ethical considerations and demonstrating specific workflows using tools like Google Stitch and Lovable.

## Key Takeaways

- **Master the five core categories** of AI design (prompting, ideation, design/prototyping, workflows, and conscious design) rather than stopping at prompting alone to create truly effective AI-generated designs.
- **Use Google Stitch with the YOLO slider** to explore divergent design directions that wouldn't emerge from traditional brainstorming, then export variants to Google AI Studio for interactive prototyping.
- **Build custom GPTs before prototyping** that ask clarifying questions about users and core needs to generate lightweight specs, then validate specs in Claude before committing Lovable credits to the final prototype.
- **Skip authentication flows in initial prototypes** by designing around the core user problem first—login becomes trivial to add later and shouldn't block early validation of your key value prop.
- **Iterate on clickable prototypes with AI tools** using annotation and feedback loops similar to Figma collaboration rather than treating the first AI output as final.
