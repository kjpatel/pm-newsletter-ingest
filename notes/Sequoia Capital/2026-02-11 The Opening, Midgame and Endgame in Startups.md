# The Opening, Midgame and Endgame in Startups

**Source**: [Sequoia Capital](https://sequoiacap.com/article/the-opening-midgame-and-endgame-in-startups/)
**Author**: Sequoia Capital | **Date**: Feb 11, 2026

---

## Summary

The best founders are "time travelers" who simultaneously play the opening, midgame, and endgame of their startups rather than progressing through them sequentially, enabling flexibility and rapid strategy adjustment. Success requires constantly balancing early-stage momentum, product-market fit execution, and long-term vision rather than focusing solely on the current phase.

## Key Takeaways

- **Play all three games at once**: When making product decisions, hiring, and investor pitches, maintain opening-phase creativity, midgame momentum, and endgame vision simultaneously rather than thinking sequentially about your business.
- **Combat structural biases**: Recognize what phase your startup is naturally engineered for (opening, midgame, or endgame) and explicitly combat the associated weaknesses—whether that's avoiding hubris in hot openings or pushing through the midgame valley of death.
- **Strengthen all narratives over time**: Each of the three startup narratives (early days vibes, traction and forward momentum, and inevitability/world-changing impact) should get stronger throughout your company's journey—don't sacrifice one for another.
- **Hire across all phases**: Even in midgame, recruit opening-style disruptors who can change direction; even in endgame, maintain hiring that emphasizes midgame execution and opening-phase reinvention.
- **Use endgame vision as a recruitment magnet**: An ambitious endgame story inspires excellent people to join and unleash their best work, making it critical to articulate even during the opening phase.
